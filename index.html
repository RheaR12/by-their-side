What is by their side?

by their side is a community project walking with Indo-Caribbean and South Asian youth in Peel Region as they navigate love, culture, and safety. We co-create workshops, conversations, and resources that make it easier to name harm early and imagine safer futures.

For youth Spaces to talk about love, pressure, boundaries, and control without judgment.
With community Collaborations with schools, families, and local organizations, not just “for” them.

by their side

We believe young people deserve relationships that feel gentle, respectful and free. Intimate partner violence doesn’t only look like bruises — it can sound like jokes, rules, guilt, and silence. Together, we’re learning how to notice those patterns early and support each other.

About the project

Rooted in community, not just in a program

This project grows out of conversations with youth, parents, and community members who wanted a softer, more honest way to talk about dating, culture, and harm.

Our story

Listening first

by their side started with young people in Peel sharing what they were carrying: pressure from family, fear of judgment, confusing ideas of “love”, and not knowing where to take their questions.

We listened, took notes, and built this work together instead of deciding for them.

Our community

Indo-Caribbean & South Asian youth in Peel

We hold space for migration stories, aunties’ comments, “what will people say?”, and how all of that sits inside relationships.

We honour the joy and care in our cultures while gently challenging the pieces that make harm easier to hide.

Our approach

Prevention as everyday practice

Our workshops are conversation-based, story-driven, and shaped by the people in the room. We talk about small choices, not just extreme situations.

For youth

If you’re wondering, “Is this normal?”

You’re allowed to have questions about love, control, and what you actually want. You don’t need a “reason big enough” to be uncomfortable.

In youth spaces, we talk about:

Love vs. control
When “caring” becomes checking your phone, choosing your clothes, or deciding who you’re allowed to see.
Coercive control
The quiet ways someone might make you feel guilty, scared, or small while calling it protection or “just how relationships are”.
Friends, group chats & screenshots
How to support each other without putting anyone at more risk.
