About

Our story

by their side grew out of quiet conversations with Indo-Caribbean and South Asian youth in Peel who were trying to make sense of love, family, and safety. This project is one way of honouring those stories and refusing to leave people alone with their questions.

Listening before planning

Before there were workshops, slides, or handouts, there were young people sharing what they were carrying: feeling watched, feeling guilty, feeling responsible for other people’s moods, and feeling unsure if what they were experiencing “counted” as harm.

We listened in classrooms, during office hours, on walks home, at community events, and in between meetings. Many of the stories sounded different on the surface, but they held the same questions: “Is this normal?”, “Do I deserve better?”, “What will my family say?”.

Rooted in Indo-Caribbean & South Asian community

Peel holds so many migration stories, aunties’ warnings, WhatsApp forwards, and unspoken family histories. It also holds joy, faith, and deep care. by their side does not ask anyone to leave their culture at the door; it asks what becomes possible when we talk honestly about the parts of our stories that make harm easier to hide.

Prevention as everyday practice

We are not a crisis line or a one-time assembly. Our work is about the little moments: a question a teacher decides to ask, a friend choosing to listen instead of tease, a parent trying a different sentence than the one they heard growing up.

Every workshop is an experiment in doing prevention differently — more grounded in community, more honest about power, and more gentle with the people in the room.
